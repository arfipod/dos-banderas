## Design Principles

### Sins

Sins should not merely punish. They should tempt.

A sin card often offers short-term power at the cost of Attachment, Desolation, or deck pollution.

### Virtues

Virtues are stable answers to sins. A virtue is useful on its own but becomes much stronger when it directly answers the sin of the current Trial.

### Gifts

Gifts increase discernment, reveal information, manipulate the round, or increase Fervor.

### Sacraments

Sacraments should feel like channels of grace. They purify, restore, strengthen, or transform failure.

### Dark Banner

World, Devil, and Flesh pressure the player in different ways:

- World: appearance, comparison, social respectability.
- Devil: accusation, confusion, false light.
- Flesh: comfort, tiredness, impulsiveness.

### Life Trials

Trials must be concrete and ordinary. The game works best when the player immediately recognizes the situation.
